Panda Bear

The panda bear, scientifically known as Ailuropoda melanoleuca, is a mammal native to China, famous for its distinctive black and white fur. These animals have a robust body and a round head, with black patches around their eyes, ears, and limbs. Their diet consists mainly of bamboo, although they occasionally consume other vegetables and small animals. Pandas are solitary and have nocturnal habits, spending much of the day resting. Despite having been endangered, conservation efforts have improved their situation, and they are now considered a vulnerable species.

Physical Characteristics

  • Bicolored fur

    • Black
  • Robust body
  • Round head
  • Strong front legs
